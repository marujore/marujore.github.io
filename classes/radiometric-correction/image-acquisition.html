Image acquisition and quantification¶

When an imaging system measure a physical process generating a digital image, the image intensity values are proportional to the energy radiated by a physical source (e.g., electromagnetic waves) measured by the sensor. Figure 3 shows the process in which electromagnetic waves (light) interacts with a scene element and is observed by an imaging system, projected into a plane and digitalized Gonzales & Woods (2007).

Figure 3 - An example of the digital image acquisition process. (a) Energy (“illumination”) source. (b) An element of a scene. (c) Imaging system. (d) Projection of the scene onto the image plane. (e) Digitized image. Source: Gonzales & Woods (2007).¶

A imagem system normally outputs continuous voltage waveform related to the amplitude and spatial behavior of the physical phenomenon being sensed. A digital image is created by converting the continuous sensed data, which involves two processes: sampling and quantization. Digitizing the coordinate values is called sampling. Digitizing the amplitude values is called quantization Gonzales & Woods (2007). This process is shown in Figure 4.

Figure 4 - Generating a digital image. (a) Continuous image. (b) A scan line from A to B in the continuous image, used to illustrate the concepts of sampling and quantization. (c) Sampling and quantization. (d) Digital scan line. Source: Gonzales & Woods (2007).¶
